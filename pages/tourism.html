Open-air cinemas in Berlin

Open-air cinema season in Berlin runs from May to September. Open-air cinemas in famous and hidden places offer a varied film program from cult films to the latest Hollywood films in the open air. All outdoor cinemas on the It is also the only OMU open-air cinema in the city. There is a different film every day this year, including highlights of the current cinema season, as well as classics and cult films. The open-air cinema is Berlin's only OmU open-air cinema and has been digitized for several seasons and thus promises visitors a significantly improved viewing experience. With films from all over the world, the Kreuzberg cinema on Mariannenplatz is cosmopolitan. As a rule, all films are shown in the original version with subtitles. Museums

Discover current exhibitions and the highlights of the collections! Berlin's Museum Island with its five museums is the largest museum ensemble in the world and is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Other museums, top exhibitions and contemporary art galleries throughout Berlin await your visit. Find even more thingst to do in our event calendar. The first Sunday of the month is free of charge at around 60 Berlin museums! These include all the buildings on Berlin's Museum Island, the Humboldt Forum, Museum für Naturkunde, Jewish Museum, Gemäldegalerie and many more. Go on a journey through time at the DDR Museum or in the German Spy Museum and see exciting exhibitions on politics and society at the German Historical Museum

Sights

  • Charlottenburg Palace: is located just out of the centre of the city. The beautiful palace hosts fine collections of china and paintings
  • The Berlin Wall Memorial: The Gedenkstätte Berliner Mauer is located between the districts of Wedding and Mitte on Bernauer Straße
  • Victory Column: Is one of the most famous landmarks of the German capital. It was erected in the 19th century and originally commemorated Germany's wars of unity. Today it has become a symbol of peace.
  • Kurfürstendamm: s the most expensive address in the capital city and home to the most exclusive brands.
  • Gendarmenmarkt: s one of the most stunning squares in the city, located close to Friedrichstraße, Berlin's exclusive shopping street in the central Mitte district

City Tours

Discover Berlin by booking a guided tour through the city. Experienced city guides show and explain sights and monuments in Berlin, lead you to the remnants of the Berlin Wall, through Berlin neighbourhoods and hip areas. Guided tours are available for different topics. Some focus on history, art and culture. Other guides offer to explore the city on foot, by bike or at a shopping tour. There is a private city tour that takes you on an individual walking tour of Berlin accompanied by a professional city guide. The tour includes a visit to Brandenburg Gate and the famous boulevards Unter den Linden and Kurfürstendamm. It will also take you to Berlin’s Government district with the Reichstag and Federal Chancellery. There are also tours on specific topics, such as the Cold War and the Berlin Wall, the Third Reich and World War II.

Events this Month

Rave the Planet Under the motto "Music is the Answer," the participants are standing up for love, peace, friendship, solidarity, diversity, respect, music, sustainability and community. The organizers' demands include the recognition of electronic music culture as a cultural asset to be protected, the abolition of dance bans and the protection of cultural venues such as clubs.
The Lesbian and Gay Festival in Berlin is Europe's largest street festival of its kind. It takes place around Nollendorfplatz, the traditional gay stomping ground in Schöneberg On Christopher Street Day (Saturday-22 July), hundreds of thousands of people take to the streets to fight for the rights of LGBTQ people with a great parade, colourful floats and lots of music.

Flea Markets

Whether you are looking for a great bargain or a unique gift, Berlin's flea markets offer a relaxed shopping experience and a great activity for your Saturday or Sunday afternoon. Browse through an endless range of items from vintage furniture and clothes to records and antiques and enjoy the pleasure of making a great bargain. Trying some of the tasty foods and drinks offered at the markets is a must-do.
The markets itself consists of mainly private vendors selling various items such as second-hand clothes, shoes, bicycles, old vinyl records, jewelry and other various knickknacks. The markets also features a large furniture tent where early birds can find true vintage treasures.

Parks

Berlin’s parks are an integral part of the city’s culture, many home to intriguing historical monuments, opportunities for outdoor activities and quintessential German beer gardens. In addition to well-known names such as Tiergarten and Tempelhofer Feld, among Berlin’s most spectacular green spaces are the lush Grunewald Forest, historic Volkspark Friedrichshain and the gardens of Charlottenburg Palace. If you want to go green, Berlin is the place to do it
